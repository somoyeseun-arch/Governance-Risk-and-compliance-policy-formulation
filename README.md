1. Policy Overview 

1.1 Purpose 
This Information Security Policy establishes Forward Microfinance Bank Ltd.’s framework for 
protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. This policy 
ensures that relevant risks are effectively identified and adequate controls are in place to ensure 
that customer and financial data and resources are adequately protected from significant threats. 
This policy offers competitive advantage by creating a structured approach to ensure protective 
measures to guard against the disclosure, alteration and destruction of customer and financial 
information. It also ensures that employees and affiliates are aware of their responsibilities, 
possess sufficient understanding of security policies, procedures, and practices, and are informed 
on how to safeguard information. 

1.2 Scope 

This policy applies to all people which include employees and vendors, processes which are 
implemented to achieve Forward Microfinance Banks organic or inorganic objectives and 
technology either automated or manual over which the entity has administrative control.
 
1.3 Objectives 

Protect sensitive financial and customer information. 
Maintain compliance with applicable regulations and standards. 
Prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction of information assets. 
Ensure business continuity through effective risk management and control 
implementation. 
Effectively manage the risks associated with security breaches or vulnerabilities. 
Ensure a secure and reliable information technology (IT) framework. 
Detect and act upon incidents involving the misuse, loss, or unauthorized access of 
information assets. 
Supervise systems for irregularities that may suggest security compromises; and 
Enhance and promote awareness of information security practices. 

1.4 Policy Statement 
Forward Microfinance Bank Ltd. Is committed to ensuring that critical IT risks are identified, 
analyzed, prioritized while implementing effective controls to treat such risks through mitigation, 
reduction, avoidance or transfer strategies in addition to continuous monitoring and staff 
awareness initiatives to ensure the establishment of a secure environment for effective protection 
of information resources. 

3. Information Security Governance 

2.1 Roles and Responsibilities 

Board of Directors: Provides oversight and approves major information security policies. 
Chief Information Security Officer (CISO): Leads the Information Security Program 
and ensures alignment with business objectives. 
Information classification officer: Implements classification of information according 
to sensitivity and handling requirements. 
Compliance officer/ Policy analyst: Ensures that the entity adheres to internal, legal and 
regulatory policies. 
IT and Security Teams: Implement security controls and monitor systems. 
Employees and Contractors: Follow established procedures and report security 
incidents promptly. 

2.2 Management Commitment 

Forward Microfinance Bank Ltd’s management shall allocate appropriate resources for security 
initiatives, risk assessments, and awareness programs. 
Evaluate and accept entity risks in line with the organizations risk appetite and risk tolerance 
levels. 
Define information security objectives which are aligned with processes to effectively identify, 
analyze, mitigate and treat risks. 
Promote security awareness culture throughout the organization by implementing and 
authorizing policies for efficient incident reporting. 
Manage information classification and protection based on best practices and legal requirements. 
Oversee information asset management, including their use and disposal, according to their 
classification. 

2.3 Legal and Regulatory Compliance 

Forward Microfinance Bank Ltd. shall comply with all applicable data protection laws (e.g. 
ISO 27001, UK GDPR, PCIDSS), financial regulations, and contractual obligations. 
Communicate policy and standards requirements, including non-compliance consequences, to 
employees and third parties, ensuring third party contract compliance. 
Informing the CISO about legal and regulatory demands or requirements. 
Complying with legal and regulatory information security obligations.

4. Access Control (Access Control Policy) 

4.1 User Access Management 

User access management implements mandatory access control according to the sensitivity level 
of information, the level of access shall depend on the sensitivity of information, access rights 
shall be granted on a least privilege and need-to-know basis, users of information shall only be 
granted access to information to execute their tasks and access shall be restricted if no longer 
required to use such data or information. User accounts access shall be authorized, documented, 
and reviewed quarterly for compliance. 

4.2 Authentication and Authorization 

All systems shall use secure authentication methods (e.g., multi-factor authentication). Privileged 
accounts must have stronger password policies and session monitoring.

5. Asset Management (Asset Management Policy) 

This Asset Management policy outlines the principles, procedures, and responsibilities 
for managing the organization's assets throughout their lifecycle. Effective asset 
management ensures optimal utilization, security, and accountability for all assets, 
contributing to the organization's operational efficiency and compliance with relevant 
regulations. 
The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured framework for acquiring, tracking, 
utilizing, maintaining, and disposing of assets. By implementing consistent asset 
management practices, the organization aims to minimize loss, reduce operational costs, 
enhance security, and maintain accurate records.  

5.1 Asset Inventory 

All hardware, software, and data assets must be identified, inventoried, and assigned an owner.

5.2 Acceptable Use 

Employees shall use Forward Microfinance Bank Ltd.’s information systems which include 
computer systems, and other end point devices responsibly and only for authorized business 
purposes, failure to comply shall lead to disciplinary measures. 

6. Physical and Environmental Security (Physical Security Policy) 

6.1 Secure Areas 
Restricted access areas such as data centers and server rooms must be restricted to authorized 
personnel only and protected with physical and operational controls such as CCTV and access 
cards to ensure access controls are adequate and effective. 

6.2 Equipment Security 

All computing equipment and end point devices shall be properly secured through a desk lock 
control to mitigate the risk of theft, pilferage or damage. 
 
7. Human Resource Security (HR Security Policy) 

7.1 Pre-Employment 
All new hires shall be required to undergo background verification and vetting in accordance 
with internal and regulatory policy to mitigate the risk of insider threats. 

7.2 Security Awareness training 

All employees shall undergo annual information security awareness training to ensure that staff are well 
abreast to recent trends of cyber threats and vulnerabilities, periodic phishing simulations shall also be 
implemented to assess the effectiveness of the trainings. 
7.3 Cyber incident management (Incident response policy) 
Forward Microfinance bank Ltd shall respond to cyber related incidents according to the standard of 
effective security response. 

All potential cybersecurity incidents or vulnerabilities shall be reported to the relevant authorities in 
accordance with the incident response plan. 
Technical issues which require digital forensics must be reported to the security operations center (SOC) 
to ensure effective containment, eradication, recovery and continuous improvement. 

8. Cryptography (Cryptography Policy) 

8.1 Encryption Standards 
Confidentiality of Customer data and financial information shall be ensured through the use of the 
advanced encryption system (AES-256), this will ensure that sensitive data either in transit or at rest is 
adequately secured from unauthorized access.  

8.2 Key Management 

Cryptographic keys shall be stored, rotated and retired in the Bitlocker or Trusted platform 
Module (TPM) built in component for the Windows platform.  

9. Operations Security (Operations Security Policy) 

9.1 Change Management 

All changes or adjustments to IT systems or technologies which may include system software migration 
or upgrade and rollback plans must be duly approved and documented by senior management.  

9.2 Malware Protection 

All endpoint devices must regularly update their antivirus and endpoint detection and response (EDR) 
software, firewall and Intrusion detection/ Prevention systems to ensure effective mitigation of cyber 
threats. 

9.3 Backup and restoration 

Critical customer and financial data shall be backed up daily at a hot site to ensure availability 
and efficient disaster recovery in case of a system downtime, this shall be tested for its 
effectiveness quarterly through simulation. 

10. Network and Communications Security ( Network Security Policy) 

10.1 Network Segmentation 

Internal networks must be segmented to separate sensitive systems from user networks. 

10.2 Firewalls and Monitoring 

The Network must be effectively monitored by implementing a next generation firewall system, 
intrusion detection/prevention systems to effectively identify and mitigate suspicious or 
malicious network activity.  

10.3 Secure Transmission 

All data in transit shall be adequately secured by implementing transport layer security (TLS) encryption 
technology, this is subject to quarterly review depending on emerging technologies and threats. 

11. Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery (Business Continuity & DR Policy)

11.1 Planning 

Forward MFB Ltd. shall maintain and test its business continuity plans for its revenue 
generating resources and the effectiveness of its disaster recovery policy for its critical IT 
infrastructure through quarterly simulation exercises to ensure service resilience. 
13. Third-Party Security (Third-Party Risk Management Policy) 

12.1 Vendor Management 

Third parties vendors offering IT infrastructure maintenance or cloud services in form of 
Infrastructure as a service (IAS), Platform as a service (PAS) or software as a service (SAS) shall 
undergo preventive controls in form of comprehensive security and due diligence checks and 
senior management approval before engagement.  
 
12.2 Data Sharing 

All confidential or sensitive data shared with vendors must be governed through a Non-disclosure 
agreement with the relevant third party to confidentiality and data protection and shall be subject to legal 
action if breached.  

13. Compliance and Audit ( Compliance Policy) 

13.1 Monitoring and Review 
The internal Audit department shall review the effectiveness and compliance with the 
Information security policy Bi-annually to ensure that significant risks are identified, and required 
controls are implemented to give reasonable assurance of achieving confidentiality, Integrity and 
availability to senior management. 

13.2 Non-Compliance 
Employees who violate this policy may face disciplinary action or legal consequences. 

14. Policy Review and Maintenance 
This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or upon significant organizational, technological, 
or regulatory change. 

15. Approval and Acknowledgement 
All employees must acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this 
Information Security Policy.
